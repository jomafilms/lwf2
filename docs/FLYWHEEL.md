# The Flywheel — How Everyone Benefits

> "We can't stop the wildfires that are upon us, but we can help you adapt to life with wildfire through thoughtful plant selection and landscape design." — Charisse Sydoriak

## The Core Insight

People don't wake up wanting a fire assessment. They wake up wanting a beautiful yard. **Plants are the gateway. Fire safety is the backbone.**

The system works when every natural behavior — browsing plants, saving favorites, buying from a nursery — ALSO generates fire safety data without the user having to think about it.

## The Flow (One Story)

### 1. Discovery: "I want plants for my yard"

A homeowner browses plants. Beautiful cards, images, nursery pricing. They're shopping.

But every plant has fire data woven in — zone badges, placement notes, risk mitigation info. They're LEARNING while they're SHOPPING. Not being lectured. Just informed.

They save plants to a list: "My front yard ideas."

**What they did:** browsed and saved.
**What the system gained:** a signal of demand by plant type and location.

### 2. Context: "What works for MY yard?"

The chat advisor asks smart questions. Not an interrogation — a conversation.
"What zone are you thinking about? Got deer? How's your irrigation?"

The user answers naturally. Every answer is saved as a preference. The recommendations get sharper.

They can also connect their address — see their property, their zones, their parcel. Optional but powerful.

**What they did:** had a conversation and maybe entered an address.
**What the system gained:** a property-level data point. Preferences. Location.

### 3. Planning: "Here's my plan"

Their saved list becomes a plan. The system shows:
- Estimated cost from local nurseries
- Which plants are in stock nearby
- How this plan affects their fire readiness
- A maintenance calendar

They can share their plan — with their HOA, with a landscaper, with their neighbor.

**What they did:** organized their wish list.
**What the system gained:** a concrete plan tied to a property.

### 4. Community: "My neighborhood is doing this"

The user can see (anonymized, opt-in):
- "Your neighborhood: 12 properties have created plans"
- "Mountain Meadows HOA preferred plants" (a curated list they can star)
- "Ashland community: 340 plans created, 23% of properties"

Nobody is called out. Nobody is shamed. The message is always: "Look what we're doing together." Progress is celebrated. Milestones are shared.

HOAs publish preferred plant lists. The city publishes zone guidelines. Users star these and build from them. **Curation creates community.**

**What they did:** looked at what their neighbors are doing.
**What the system gained:** social proof. Participation momentum.

### 5. Commerce: "I'm ready to buy"

From their plan, they can see: "Shooting Star Nursery has 80% of your plants in stock." One click to view at the nursery. Eventually, one click to order.

The nursery sees demand: "47 users in Ashland have Oregon Grape on their plans." They stock accordingly. They update their inventory because it drives sales.

Landscapers see opportunity: "8 plans in Mountain Meadows need professional installation." They offer services.

**What they did:** bought plants or hired a landscaper.
**What the system gained:** transactions. Revenue potential. Proof of impact.

### 6. Progress: "We're getting there"

The city dashboard shows community progress. Not "compliance" — **readiness.** 

"Ashland fire readiness: 35% of properties have plans. Up from 12% last quarter."

This data goes to:
- City council for budget discussions
- Grant applications for federal/state funding
- Insurance companies for community-level rate consideration
- Bond markets for infrastructure financing (Woolsey's vision)

**What everyone did:** followed their natural behavior.
**What the community gained:** measurable progress toward the 90% goal.

## Why Each Participant Comes Back

|-----|-------------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **Homeowner** | Beautiful yard, save money | Plans aligned with fire zones | Seasonal updates, new plants, maintenance reminders |
| **Landscaper** | Find clients, showcase work | Designs meet compliance | Client leads, demand visibility, certification |
| **Nursery** | Sell plants | Stock what communities need | Demand signals, featured placement, direct orders |
| **HOA** | Maintain property values | CC&R compliance | Progress tracking, member engagement |
| **City** | Community safety | Measurable risk reduction | Grant data, budget justification, 90% goal tracking |

## Privacy Principles

- **Opt-in everything.** Nobody's data is shared without explicit consent.
- **Neighborhood data is aggregate only.** "12 properties" not "the Smiths haven't done theirs."
- **No shaming.** Always: "Look how far we've come" not "Look who's behind."
- **Users control their visibility.** Private lists by default. Share when ready.
- **Socially vulnerable populations welcome.** Free tier covers everything a homeowner needs. Cost should never be a barrier to fire safety information.

## Language (from Charisse)

- "Choosing plants for fire-prone yards" — not "fire-safe landscaping tool"
- "Fire readiness" — not "compliance"  
- "Risk mitigation" — not "risk assessment"
- "fire-reluctant" or "fire-resistant" — NEVER "fire-resilient"
- "Informed choices" — not "approved plants"
- The 5 questions: Placement, Spacing, Laddering, Maintenance, Flammability

## What Makes This Different

Every other tool is a plant picker OR a compliance checker OR a map tool.

This is the connective tissue. The Charisse database — 1,361 plants, 100+ sources, placement codes, risk reduction rules — is the moat nobody else has. The community system around it — lists, plans, neighborhoods, nursery demand signals — is what turns a database into a movement.

**Value creates value. Everyone benefits. Nobody loses.**
